## Part 1: Forced-Choice Questions (20 items)

### TRA_01 (Transfer)
> You're stuck on a problem. You could try applying an approach from a different subject, but it might not work and you'd lose time. Or you could look up the specific method.
> - A: Try the cross-subject approach first. Even if it fails, I might learn something.
> - B: Look up the right method. No point reinventing the wheel.

**a_indicates:** high_transfer
**b_indicates:** low_transfer

---

### TRA_02 (Transfer)
> You've just learned something new and it clicks. What happens next?
> - A: I start noticing how it connects to other things I already know.
> - B: I focus on practising it until I've properly locked it in.

**a_indicates:** high_transfer
**b_indicates:** low_transfer

---

### REC_01 (Receptivity)
> Your mock results are lower than expected. A teacher offers to go through your paper with you.
> - A: Take them up on it. Quicker than figuring it out alone.
> - B: Look through it myself first. I'll ask if I get stuck.

**a_indicates:** high_receptivity
**b_indicates:** low_receptivity

---

### REC_02 (Receptivity)
> You've finished a draft of your personal statement. A family member offers to read it and give honest feedback.
> - A: Let them read it. Another perspective might catch something I can't see.
> - B: Hold off. I'd rather get it to a place I'm happy with first.

**a_indicates:** high_receptivity
**b_indicates:** low_receptivity

---

### TOL_01 (Tolerance)
> You've been stuck on a problem for 20 minutes with no progress. You could keep going or move on and come back later.
> - A: Keep going. Sometimes it clicks right after the frustrating bit.
> - B: Move on. I'll come back with fresh eyes.

**a_indicates:** high_tolerance
**b_indicates:** low_tolerance

---

### TOL_02 (Tolerance)
> You're working through a difficult topic and it's not making sense. You could push through the chapter or switch to something easier and return tomorrow.
> - A: Push through. I don't like leaving things half-understood.
> - B: Switch for now. Forcing it rarely works for me.

**a_indicates:** high_tolerance
**b_indicates:** low_tolerance

---

### PRE_01 (Precision)
> You've finished an assignment due in 20 minutes and spot a small inconsistency near the end. Fixing it properly would take 15 minutes, leaving no time to check anything else.
> - A: Fix it. I'd rather submit with one thing done properly.
> - B: Leave it and check the rest. One small error probably matters less than something bigger I haven't spotted.

**a_indicates:** high_precision
**b_indicates:** low_precision

---

### PRE_02 (Precision)
> You're in an exam with 10 minutes left and 3 questions unanswered.
> - A: Focus on one and answer it properly.
> - B: Attempt all three at surface level. Partial marks add up.

**a_indicates:** high_precision
**b_indicates:** low_precision

### CAL_01 (Calibration)

> - B: I say I don't know. I've been wrong too many times.

**a_indicates:** high_calibration
**b_indicates:** low_calibration

### CAL_02 (Calibration)

**a_indicates:** low_calibration
**b_indicates:** high_calibration

> - B: Move on. I'll come back to it after exams if I'm still curious.

**a_indicates:** high_depth
**b_indicates:** low_depth

### STR_01 (Structure)

**a_indicates:** high_structure

### STR_02 (Structure)

**a_indicates:** high_structure
**b_indicates:** low_structure

**a_indicates:** high_social
**b_indicates:** low_social

> - B: Decline. I cover more ground on my own.

**a_indicates:** high_social

### CON_01 (Consistency)

> - A: Do some work. I like staying on top of things even when there's no pressure.
> - B: Take it off. I work best when there's a real deadline.

**a_indicates:** high_consistency
**b_indicates:** low_consistency

---

### CON_02 (Consistency)
> Your productivity varies a lot day to day. Someone suggests a fixed daily schedule.
> - A: Worth trying. I'd probably get more done overall.
> - B: Wouldn't suit me. I work in bursts and that's fine.

**a_indicates:** high_consistency
**b_indicates:** low_consistency

---

### RET_01 (Retrieval)
> You're revising a topic you covered last month. You could reread your notes or test yourself without looking.
> - A: Test myself. It's harder but I'll know what I've actually forgotten.
> - B: Reread first. I want to refresh my memory before testing.

**a_indicates:** high_retrieval
**b_indicates:** low_retrieval

### STEM_TECH_01 (STEM-Technical)

**Meta-cluster:** Computing, Engineering, Maths
**Format:** MCQ
**Time limit:** 90 seconds

**Stimulus:**
> A student builds an automatic plant watering system:
> - Moisture sensor checks soil every hour
> - If soil is dry, pump activates for 10 seconds
> - Pump draws water from tank
> - Tank has a float sensor that shows empty/not empty
> - If tank is empty, warning light turns on and pump won't activate
>
> Problem: The soil is dry, the tank is full, but the pump never activates. The warning light is off.

**Question:** Which component is most likely faulty?

### HUM_01 (Humanities)

**Meta-cluster:** History, Languages, Philosophy, English
**Format:** MCQ
**Time limit:** 90 seconds

**Stimulus:**
> A historian writes:
>
> "The factory workers of 1830s Manchester lived in appalling conditions. Contemporary accounts describe overcrowded housing, poor sanitation, and widespread disease. This proves that industrialisation was a disaster for the working class."

**Question:** A tutor asks: "What's the problem with this argument?"

**Options:**
- A) The sources might be biased. Reformers had reasons to exaggerate.
- B) "Appalling" is subjective. Conditions might have been normal for the time.
- C) It only describes Manchester. Other industrial cities might have been different.
- D) It doesn't compare to conditions before industrialisation. Workers might have been worse off in rural areas.

**Correct answer:** D

**Follow-up questions:**
1. How did you find that? (üò´ Frustrating | üòê Neutral | üòä Enjoyed it)

### SOC_SCI_01 (Social Sciences)

**Meta-cluster:** Psychology, Sociology, Politics, Economics
**Format:** Short response
**Time limit:** 90 seconds

**Stimulus:**
> Research shows that employees who work from home report higher job satisfaction. A company mandates work-from-home for all staff to improve morale.

**Question:** In one sentence, why might this backfire?

**Response type:** Text box

**AI grading criteria:**

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0 | No reasoning or misses the point |
| 1 | Vague ("not everyone likes working from home") |
| 2 | Identifies self-selection (people who chose it liked it, forcing is different) |
| 3 | Explains self-selection AND considers who loses out (extroverts, new staff, those with poor home setups) |

**Follow-up questions:**
1. How did you find that? (üò´ Frustrating | üòê Neutral | üòä Enjoyed it)

**Time limit:** 90 seconds

### CREA_01 (Creative)

**Meta-cluster:** Art, Design, Media, Architecture
**Format:** Short response
**Time limit:** 90 seconds

**Stimulus:**
> You're designing a poster for a charity event. The client wants it to be:
>
> 1. Eye-catching
> 2. Include all event details
> 3. Feel welcoming and not overwhelming

> You can only fully achieve two.

### Mini-Sample Table Schema

```sql
CREATE TABLE bo_v1_mini_samples (
    id TEXT PRIMARY KEY,
    meta_cluster TEXT NOT NULL,
    cluster_covers TEXT[] NOT NULL,
    format TEXT NOT NULL CHECK (format IN ('MCQ', 'SHORT_RESPONSE')),
    time_limit_seconds INTEGER DEFAULT 90,
    stimulus TEXT NOT NULL,
    question TEXT NOT NULL,
    options JSONB,
    correct_answer TEXT,
    grading_criteria JSONB,
    created_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT NOW()
);

CREATE TABLE bo_v1_mini_sample_responses (
    id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
    session_id UUID REFERENCES bo_v1_sessions(id),
    mini_sample_id TEXT REFERENCES bo_v1_mini_samples(id),
    response TEXT NOT NULL,
    score INTEGER,
    time_taken_seconds INTEGER,
    enjoyment_rating INTEGER CHECK (enjoyment_rating IN (-1, 0, 1)),
    career_fit_rating INTEGER CHECK (career_fit_rating IN (-1, 0, 1)),
    created_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT NOW()
);
```
